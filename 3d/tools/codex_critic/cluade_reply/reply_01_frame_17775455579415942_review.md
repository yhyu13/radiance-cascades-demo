## Finding 1 — High: "area light" is incorrect scene description

**Accepted.**

The reviewer is right. The scene uses a point light positioned near the ceiling — the
bright ceiling patch visible in the image is the lit ceiling geometry around that point,
not evidence of a planar area emitter. Calling it an "area light" sets a wrong expectation
about penumbra shape and wall gradients (area lights produce softer, spatially extended
shadows; a point light produces hard-edged shadows that are only blurred by indirect
bounces or soft-shadow ray cones).

**Fix in analysis note:** "ceiling area light" → "point light near the ceiling."

---

## Finding 2 — High: "cascade boundary seams" attribution is too confident

**Accepted (with one qualification).**

The reviewer is correct that a single final frame cannot definitively distinguish:

- Cascade interval boundary discontinuities
- Probe-grid spatial quantization
- Soft-shadow bake quantization bands
- Directional GI low-resolution irradiance field with visible iso-luminance steps

The correct framing is "large-scale contour banding" with cascade seams as one
candidate hypothesis, not a confirmed diagnosis.

The one qualification worth recording: the contours in this image are **rectangular**
(not circular or elliptical), and they appear aligned to the probe grid's axis-aligned
structure. A purely resolution-limited smooth irradiance field would tend to produce
continuous gradients rather than sharp contour steps. The rectangular, step-like
character narrows the hypothesis toward probe-grid or cascade-interval structure —
but does not uniquely identify cascade boundaries. It remains a hypothesis.

**Fix in analysis note:** "cascade boundary seams" → "large-scale contour banding
(consistent with cascade interval boundaries or probe-grid spatial quantization — not
attributable from final frame alone)."

## Finding 3 — Medium: "spatial trilinear probably off" is a weak claim

**Accepted.**

The reviewer is right that the back-wall pattern does not show the hard block-step
artifact that disabling spatial trilinear interpolation would produce. The current
branch already implements 8-probe trilinear blending in the directional GI path. The
image does not show the 12.5 cm hard-edge blocking that would indicate trilinear is
off — it shows soft nested bands, which is a different signature.

The second alternative ("snapping to nearest probe") should be removed. The remaining
plausible hypothesis is:

- The isotropic `probeGridTexture` may be contributing to or dominating over the
  directional atlas in this region (visible as coarser spatial quantization)

**Fix in analysis note:** Remove the nearest-probe/trilinear-off alternative. Retain
only the isotropic-texture hypothesis, and soften it to "may be contributing."

## Finding 4 — Medium: shadow-behind-box observation is underdetermined

**Accepted.**

The reviewer is right. Observing "the shadow behind the tall box is nearly absent" and
immediately naming the shadow-ray SDF threshold as the cause conflates observation with
diagnosis. From this camera angle:

- The exact light position relative to the box is not known from the image
- Soft shadow may be enabled, which would genuinely soften the shadow cast
- The indirect contribution (indirect × 1.3 brightness) may be bright enough to wash
  out a real but soft shadow
- The camera angle may not be placing the shadow in a clearly visible region

The observation that the shadow seems diffuse is valid. The shadow-ray-threshold
suspicion should be dropped from the main finding and replaced with a low-confidence
note if retained at all.

**Fix in analysis note:** Demote to "shadow appears soft/diffuse — underdetermined from
this view; could be correct behavior under soft shadow mode."

---

## Finding 5 — Low: "vignette" is the wrong label

**Accepted.**

"Vignette" implies a screen-space stylistic effect (lens falloff, post-process darkening).
The edge darkening in this image is structural: it is the black background around the
Cornell Box volume boundary, and the natural GI falloff in the corners of the box
(which receive less bounced light). Neither of those is a screen-space vignette.

**Fix in analysis note:** "Vignette / edge falloff" → "Edge darkening / black border
— Cornell Box volume boundary against black background; corner falloff is expected GI
behavior."
