## Artifacts

### 1. Cascade boundary seams — ceiling and back wall (HIGH)

Concentric rectangular contour lines clearly visible on the back wall and ceiling, radiating outward from the ceiling light source. These are ring-shaped iso-distance discontinuities at cascade level boundaries — indirect radiance jumps at each cascade interval transition.

**Geometry-coupled cause:** The contour spacing matches the cascade interval doubling (C0→C1→C2→C3). This strongly indicates the merge blend at cascade boundaries is not smooth — the bilinear/blend weight at the cascade boundary transitions abruptly to 0 or 1 instead of blending continuously.

---

### 2. Probe-grid banding — back wall and ceiling (MEDIUM)

Superimposed on the cascade seams is a finer regular rectangular grid pattern across the back wall and parts of the ceiling. The grid cell size corresponds to the C0 probe spacing.

**Geometry-coupled cause:** The cell period matching C0 probe spacing (~12.5 cm) indicates the isotropic reduction texture (`probeGridTexture`) is contributing or dominating in regions where the directional atlas (`probeAtlasTexture`) should be used. Alternatively, spatial trilinear interpolation between probes is not engaged and the renderer is snapping to nearest probe.

---

### 3. Vignette / edge falloff (LOW)

The entire scene fades to black at the edges of the render area with a wide vignette. Likely a raymarch ray termination or volume clip issue — rays near grazing angles exit the SDF volume boundary early and return zero radiance. Not a GI artifact per se.

---

### 4. Soft/missing shadow from ceiling light (LOW)

The boxes cast soft contact shadows on the floor, but the ceiling light does not produce a well-defined shadow behind the left (taller) box on the back wall. The shadow is diffuse to the point of being nearly absent. If shadow rays are enabled, the SDF shadow ray threshold (`t_min` or normal offset) may be too large, causing the ray to skip past the occluder.
